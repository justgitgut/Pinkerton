How to use

1. Log in to pinalove.com — Pinkerton opens it automatically when you click the extension icon.

2. Go to Settings, set your scan rate (1–5 min) and filters (gender, age range), then press ▶ Start Automatic Scanning.

3. Click ⊞ Open Interface in the header to open the full results page.

What it does

Pinkerton tracks PinaLove activity over time and turns raw browsing into clear signals. All data stays local in your browser.

It can help reduce scam risk by highlighting suspicious patterns (for example, profiles that appear online almost all the time, or very old profiles with unusual behaviour) so you can decide more carefully.

It also helps you catch new members early, including users who are online only briefly, while giving you faster navigation than the default PinaLove flow.

You get live online/new/premium/verified stats, 24h and monthly activity views, profile change tracking, photo insights, and smoother profile-to-profile browsing.

Important

Browser must be running: Pinkerton can collect data only while your browser is open.

Your own timeline: Pinkerton builds its dataset from your first run onward, based on your scans and usage history.

Local-only storage: all scan data, settings, and analytics are stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to remote servers.

Scan settings

Scan Rate — how often Pinkerton fetches online users (1 to 5 minutes).

Gender — filter scans to Women, Men, or Both.

Age range — narrow the scanned population by minimum and maximum age.

Auto-scan — runs in the background even when the popup is closed. Toggle it off to pause.

Interface features

Search & filters — search by username, filter by membership type, sort by last seen, name, or join date.

Card view — click any card to open the profile modal; click the username to visit the PinaLove profile page.

Table view — same behaviour, lazy-loaded for speed.

Profile modal — shows photos, bio, field history, 24h sparkline, and a monthly activity chart. Navigate between profiles with Prev/Next.
